SURFACE DESCENT

The surface was a lie.

For three hundred years, the Architects built their cities above ground — gleaming, towering, blind. They told their children the earth had no bottom. They burned the maps that said otherwise.

They were wrong.

* * *

Deep beneath the crust, older than any civilization, five chambers were carved by hands that had no name. Each one sealed with a Crystal — not to protect what was inside, but to protect us from it.

The Hollow King was buried there.

Not dead. Not sleeping.
Waiting.

* * *

The Architects cracked the first seal thirty years ago. They called it an excavation. They called it progress. Seventeen of them descended into the shaft.

None of them came back up.

The shaft was sealed. The records were destroyed. The city above it was abandoned overnight — no announcement, no explanation, just silence where a million people used to be.

* * *

Now the seals are failing.

One by one, the five chambers are breaking open from the inside. The ground shakes. The sky above the dead cities has turned the color of an old wound.

He is almost free.

* * *

You are the last Descender.

Not chosen. Not special. You are here because everyone else is already gone, and someone has to go back down.

Five chambers. Five crystals. Each one guarded by something that has had centuries to get very, very good at killing.

Collect them all.
Open the final gate.
Finish what the Architects were too afraid to start.

* * *

The journey starts now.

What comes next is beaneath the surface.
